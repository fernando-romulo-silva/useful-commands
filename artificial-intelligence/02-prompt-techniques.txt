01 Zero-Shot Prompting

Large language model (LLM) is instructed to perform a task without being given any examples or prior demonstrations
Zero-shot prompting is best for simple, common tasks where the model likely already understands the context.

Common Use Cases:

* Sentiment Analysis: "Classify the sentiment of this review as positive or negative".

* Summarization: "Summarize the following text in two sentences".

* Translation: "Translate 'Hello' to Spanish".

* Text Classification: "Categorize this email as spam or not spam" IBM.

02 Few-shot prompting

Prompt engineering technique where a model is provided with a few examples (demonstrations) of input-output pairs within the prompt to improve accuracy, guide the tone, and dictate formatting.

Common Use Cases:

* When the task requires high precision;

* Generating text with a defined format;

* In categorizations with a lot of variety;

* Tasks with complex rules.


Define the task: Briefly explain what you want the AI to do.
Provide examples: Give 2–5 examples showing inputs and desired outputs, according to PromptHub and Mem0.
Provide the target input: Ask the AI to complete the final example. 

Ex:

A "whatpu" is a small, furry animal native to Tanzania. 
An example of a sentence that uses the word whatpu is:
We were traveling in Africa and we saw these very cute whatpus.
 
To do a "farduddle" means to jump up and down really fast.

03 Chain of Thought

Technique that improves Large Language Model (LLM) performance on complex tasks—such as math, logic, and reasoning—by encouraging the model to generate intermediate, step-by-step reasoning steps before providing a final answer.

Examples include adding "Let's think step by step" to queries, providing examples with intermediate reasoning steps

Ex:

A farmer has 17 sheep. All but 9 die. How many are left?

Think through this step by step:
1. What does "all but 9" mean?
2. What happens to those 9?
3. How many are left?
